# Team Evolution Rules

> Stability: MEDIUM-HIGH — higher threshold than replanning. Team structure changes have broad impact.

---

## When Team Evolution Is Permitted

Team evolution (modifying team-roster.md) is only permitted when:

1. A capability gap has been identified that is causing measurable risk
2. The gap cannot be addressed by adjusting how existing roles operate
3. A checkpoint review has occurred (team-roster.md is not modified mid-checkpoint)
4. The Orchestrator has formally approved the proposal

---

## Proposal Format

Any Lead may propose a team evolution. The proposal must be written and include:

```
## Team Evolution Proposal

Proposer: [role name]
Date: [date]
Checkpoint: [checkpoint number]

### Capability Gap Identified
[What specific capability is missing or overloaded?]

### Risk Caused by Gap
[What has gone wrong or could go wrong because of this gap?]

### Proposed Change
[Add role / remove role / split role / merge roles — be specific]

### Does This Meet Evolution Criteria?
[Cite the rule above that permits this change]

### Alternatives Considered
[What else was tried or considered before proposing team evolution?]
```

Submit the proposal to the Orchestrator. The Orchestrator decides: approve or reject.

---

## Orchestrator Decision

The Orchestrator reviews the proposal against these rules:

- Is the gap real (evidenced, not speculative)?
- Is the proposed change the minimum necessary to address the gap?
- Will the change create new coordination overhead that outweighs the benefit?

**If approved:** Update team-roster.md and record in decision-log.md.

**If rejected:** Record the rejection reasoning in decision-log.md. The proposer may resubmit with new evidence at a future checkpoint.

---

## What Team Evolution May Change

**May change:**
- Add a new role to team-roster.md
- Remove a role that is no longer needed
- Split one role into two specialized roles
- Merge two roles into one

**May not change:**
- The Orchestrator role (this role is always present)
- The fundamental governance structure (that requires human intervention)
